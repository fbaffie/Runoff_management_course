Erik

  • How can livestock farming influence flooding?
  • Livestock farming (in particular: high density) can lead to soil compaction, which in turn decreases infiltration capacity. According to a model study, this practice gave a 7 % increase in flood peak after a storm. In another study, a plot-scale experiment, grazed (by sheep) plots were compared to tree-planted plots and found to produce 48%-78% more run-off. After five years, infiltration rates were 67 times higher.
